Major lenders

  • National Bank of Egypt (NBE) — largest state bank, broad mortgage access
  • Banque Misr — wide branch network, government housing schemes

  • QNB Alahli — flexible tenures

Subsidized vs commercial mortgages

  • CBE Mortgage Finance Initiative — a subsidized rate near 8% for eligible low and middle-income buyers, a fraction of commercial cost. Strict income and unit-price limits apply.
  • Commercial mortgages — 22–26% per year, tracking the CBE overnight rate (19%). Expensive, so larger down payments and shorter tenures matter a lot.
  • Always compare the subsidized route first — the difference over 15 years is enormous.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the mortgage installment calculated in Egypt?

Using the reducing-balance formula P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1). Commercial mortgage rates float with the CBE policy rate, while subsidized-initiative loans use a fixed low rate.

What is the current mortgage rate in Egypt?

Commercial mortgage rates are roughly 22–26% per year in 2026, reflecting the CBE overnight deposit rate of 19%. The subsidized CBE initiative offers around 8% for eligible buyers.

Who qualifies for the CBE subsidized mortgage?

Low and middle-income Egyptians buying within set unit-price and income limits. The subsidized ~8% rate is far cheaper than commercial loans — apply through participating banks like NBE and Banque Misr.

How much down payment is required?

Typically 20–30% for commercial mortgages. Subsidized programs may allow lower down payments within their caps. A larger down payment sharply reduces total interest at these rates.

Is the high commercial rate worth it?

At 22–26%, a commercial mortgage roughly triples the cost of the home over 15 years. Most buyers should exhaust the subsidized initiative and maximize their down payment first.
