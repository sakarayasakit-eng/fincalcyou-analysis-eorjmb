How the comparison works

For buying, it adds up your down payment, loan payments and upkeep over your stay, then subtracts the home's value after price growth — that net figure is your true cost of owning. For renting, it totals every year's rent (with yearly increases). The lower net cost wins.

It's not only about money

The math often favors buying over long stays and renting over short ones — but flexibility, maintenance hassle and job mobility matter too. Use the number as a strong input, not the whole decision.
