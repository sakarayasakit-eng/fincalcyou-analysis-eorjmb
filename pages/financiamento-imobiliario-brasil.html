Frequently Asked Questions

How is the financiamento payment calculated in Brazil?

Lenders use the reducing-balance formula P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1), where P is the loan, r the monthly rate and n the number of months. The fin·calc tool applies the same formula with Brazil's typical rate.

What is the current home loan rate in Brazil?

As a 2026 reference, typical rates are around 12.0% per year, linked to the Banco Central do Brasil (BCB) policy rate. SBPE financing ≈ 10–12% p.a., SFH (lower income) ≈ 8–10%. Free market loans ≈ 13–16%. Selic rate 15%. Min 20% down typical. Providers: Caixa, Banco do Brasil, Itaú, Bradesco, Santander.

How much deposit do I need?

A larger down payment lowers both your rate and total interest, and reduces the lender's risk. Most lenders also add valuation, legal and registration costs on top of the deposit.

Can I reduce the total interest?

Yes — a bigger deposit, a shorter term, and extra or early repayments all cut total interest significantly. Use the prepayment option in the calculator to see your exact saving.
