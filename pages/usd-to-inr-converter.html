Tips to get more INR for your money

  • Compare the total INR received for your exact amount — providers price differently at $200 vs $2,000.
  • Watch the exchange-rate margin, not just the upfront fee; that's where most cost hides.
  • First-transfer promo rates (Remitly, Xoom) can beat everyone once — but check the ongoing rate too.
  • Bank transfers to an NRE/NRO account are convenient but usually carry the widest margin.
  • Send larger, less frequent transfers to spread fixed fees over more rupees.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the live USD to INR rate today?

Rates change constantly, so use the converter above for the current mid-market USD/INR rate. The mid-market rate is the true rate; the amount you receive depends on each provider's margin and fees.

What is the cheapest way to send USD to India?

For transparency and low total cost, Wise (mid-market rate) and Remitly are usually strong. Western Union suits cash pickup. Always compare the total INR received for your exact amount — that's what matters.

Why does the rate I get differ from Google's rate?

Google shows the mid-market rate. Money-transfer providers add a margin to that rate and may charge a fee, so your effective rate is slightly worse. Compare the final INR amount across providers.

Is it better to use a bank or a money-transfer service?

Specialist services (Wise, Remitly) almost always beat banks on the exchange-rate margin for India remittances. Banks are convenient for NRE/NRO deposits but usually cost the most.

How can I get the best USD–INR rate?

Compare total received across two or three providers, use first-transfer promos where available, watch the margin not just the fee, and send larger amounts less often to dilute fixed fees.
