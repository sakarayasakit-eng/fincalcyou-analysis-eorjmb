How to get the most rupees for your dollars

  • Compare the amount received, not the fee. A "zero fee" transfer can still cost you 3% in a poor exchange rate.
  • Mid-market rate is the benchmark. The converter shows it live — anything a provider gives you below it is their margin.
  • Specialist services (Wise, Remitly) usually beat bank wires for everyday amounts.
  • NRE/NRO transfers for larger sums: weigh a bank's flat wire fee against a percentage fee.
  • Timing matters on large transfers — even a 1% rate move on $10,000 is ₹8,000+.

Ways to send USD to India

  • Wise — mid-market rate, low transparent fee; great for most transfers.
  • Remitly — fast/express options; promotional first-transfer rates.
  • Bank wire (SWIFT) — reliable for large amounts but usually the widest rate margin.
  • NRE/NRO account transfers — for NRIs repatriating funds; check tax treatment.

Always run the exact amount through the live converter first so you know the true mid-market value before a provider's margin.

Frequently Asked Questions

Best way to send money to India?

Usually a specialist (Wise/Remitly) for everyday amounts — compare the final INR received, not the fee.

How is the USD-INR rate set?

The mid-market rate is the real interbank rate; providers add a margin on top. The converter shows the live mid-market figure.

Is money sent to India taxed?

Generally not as income for the recipient, but interest earned can be. Check current Indian tax rules for your case.
