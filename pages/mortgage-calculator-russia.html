Frequently Asked Questions

How is the mortgage payment calculated in Russia?

Lenders use the reducing-balance formula P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1), where P is the loan, r the monthly rate and n the number of months. The fin·calc tool applies the same formula with Russia's typical rate.

What is the current home loan rate in Russia?

As a 2026 reference, typical rates are around 22.0% per year, linked to the Bank of Russia (CBR) policy rate. Mortgage rates ≈ 20–24% p.a. (market). Subsidized "Family Mortgage" programme ≈ 6% for qualifying families. CBR key rate 15%. Providers: Sberbank, VTB, Gazprombank, Alfa-Bank.

How much deposit do I need?

A larger down payment lowers both your rate and total interest, and reduces the lender's risk. Most lenders also add valuation, legal and registration costs on top of the deposit.

Can I reduce the total interest?

Yes — a bigger deposit, a shorter term, and extra or early repayments all cut total interest significantly. Use the prepayment option in the calculator to see your exact saving.
