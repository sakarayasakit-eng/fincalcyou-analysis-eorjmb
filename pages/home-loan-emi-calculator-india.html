The real cost of a ₹50 lakh home loan

Example: ₹50,00,000 loan at 8.5% for 20 years.

  • Monthly EMI: ₹43,391
  • Total paid back over 20 years: ≈ ₹1.04 crore
  • Total interest: ≈ ₹54.1 lakh
  • So you repay roughly 2.08× the amount you borrowed.

Tip: A small prepayment early in the tenure removes years of interest. Even one extra EMI a year can shave 3–4 years off a 20-year loan. Use the prepayment slider to see your exact saving.

How to get the lowest home loan rate

  • CIBIL score 750+ unlocks the best advertised rates — check and fix your report first.
  • Higher down payment (lower LTV) reduces both your rate and total interest.
  • Compare the EBLR spread, not just the headline rate — banks add a fixed margin over the repo.
  • Women co-applicants often get a 0.05% concession at several banks.
  • Balance transfer — if your existing rate is above 9%, switching lenders can save lakhs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is home loan EMI calculated?

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1), where P is principal, r the monthly rate, and n the months. Every Indian bank uses this same reducing-balance formula.

What is the current home loan rate in India?

Roughly 7.75%–9% p.a. as of April 2026, floating with the RBI repo (5.25%). Your score and profile decide the exact rate.

What is the maximum tenure?

Up to 30 years at most banks, subject to your age at maturity. Longer tenure = lower EMI but much higher total interest.

Prepay or invest?

Prepaying saves a guaranteed ~8.5%. Investing may earn more (Nifty long-term ≈12%) but with risk. The calculator compares both with your numbers.

Are there tax benefits?

Under the old regime, Section 24(b) gives up to ₹2 lakh on interest and 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh on principal. Confirm with a tax advisor.
