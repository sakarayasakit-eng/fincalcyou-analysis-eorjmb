Frequently Asked Questions

How is the mortgage payment calculated in Taiwan?

Lenders use the reducing-balance formula P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1), where P is the loan, r the monthly rate and n the number of months. The fin·calc tool applies the same formula with Taiwan's typical rate.

What is the current home loan rate in Taiwan?

As a 2026 reference, typical rates are around 2.2% per year, linked to the Central Bank of the Republic of China (CBC) policy rate. Mortgage rates ≈ 2.0–2.5% p.a. CBC discount rate 2.00% (Mar 2026, held since Mar 2024). Major banks: Bank of Taiwan, Mega International, CTBC, E.Sun, Cathay United, Taishin, Fubon, First Bank.

How much deposit do I need?

A larger down payment lowers both your rate and total interest, and reduces the lender's risk. Most lenders also add valuation, legal and registration costs on top of the deposit.

Can I reduce the total interest?

Yes — a bigger deposit, a shorter term, and extra or early repayments all cut total interest significantly. Use the prepayment option in the calculator to see your exact saving.
