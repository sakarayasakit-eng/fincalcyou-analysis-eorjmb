The real cost of a UK mortgage

Example: £300,000 loan at 4.50% for 25 years repayment.

  • Monthly payment: £1,668
  • Total paid over 25 years: £500,346
  • Total interest: £200,346
  • Stamp duty on a £400k home (non-first-time buyer): £7,500
  • Conveyancing + survey: £1,500 – £3,000
  • Mortgage arrangement fee: typically £999 – £1,999 (added to loan or paid upfront)

Budget for 1–2% of purchase price in upfront costs beyond the deposit.

First-time buyer schemes (2026)

  • Lifetime ISA (LISA) — save up to £4,000/yr, government adds 25% (£1,000 max). Available up to age 39, use toward first home up to £450,000.
  • Stamp Duty relief — First-time buyers pay no stamp duty on homes up to £425,000 (savings up to £11,250).
  • First Homes scheme — 30–50% discount on selected new-build homes for key workers, first-time buyers, and local residents.
  • Shared Ownership — buy 25–75% of a home, pay rent on the rest. Good for high-cost areas like London.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current UK mortgage rate?

As of April 2026, typical fixed rates range from 4.2% to 5.5%. BoE base rate is 4.00%. The cheapest deals are for 40% deposit.

2-year or 5-year fixed?

2-year fixes are typically lower but you face remortgaging in 2 years. 5-year fixes are 0.1–0.3% higher but give longer certainty. With rates expected to drift lower, many borrowers are choosing 2-year.

How much deposit do I need?

Minimum 5% (95% LTV), but cheapest rates require 40% deposit (60% LTV). First-time buyers can use LISA, First Homes, or shared ownership.

What about stamp duty?

In England/NI: 0% up to £250,000 (£425,000 first-time buyers), then 5% up to £925,000, 10% up to £1.5m. Second homes and buy-to-let pay extra 5%.

Repayment or interest-only?

Repayment is standard — loan is cleared by term end. Interest-only has lower monthly payments but full capital is owed at term end. Common for buy-to-let, rare for residential.
