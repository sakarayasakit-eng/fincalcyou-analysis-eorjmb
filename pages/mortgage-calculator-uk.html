Budget for 1–2% of purchase price in upfront costs beyond the deposit.

First-time buyer schemes (2026)

  • Lifetime ISA (LISA) — save up to £4,000/yr, government adds 25% (£1,000 max), for a first home up to £450,000.
  • Stamp Duty relief — first-time buyers pay no stamp duty up to £425,000.
  • First Homes scheme — 30–50% discount on selected new-builds for key workers and locals.
  • Shared Ownership — buy 25–75% of a home, pay rent on the rest.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current UK mortgage rate?

As of April 2026, typical fixed rates range from 4.2% to 5.5%. BoE base rate is 4.00%. The cheapest deals are for 40% deposit.

2-year or 5-year fixed?

2-year fixes are typically lower but you remortgage in 2 years. 5-year fixes are 0.1–0.3% higher but give longer certainty.

How much deposit do I need?

Minimum 5% (95% LTV), but cheapest rates require 40% deposit. First-time buyers can use LISA, First Homes, or shared ownership.

What about stamp duty?

In England/NI: 0% up to £250,000 (£425,000 first-time buyers), then 5% up to £925,000, 10% up to £1.5m. Second homes pay extra 5%.

Repayment or interest-only?

Repayment clears the loan by term end. Interest-only has lower payments but full capital is owed at the end. Rare for residential.
