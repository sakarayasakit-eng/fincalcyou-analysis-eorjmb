  • Monthly payment: S$4,005

  • Total interest: S$401,497
  • An HDB concessionary loan at 2.6% on the same amount costs noticeably less in total interest.

HDB loan vs bank loan, and TDSR

  • HDB concessionary loan (2.6%) — only for eligible HDB flats; stable rate, higher LTV. Bank loans (≈3–3.5%) — for private property or by choice, SORA-linked or fixed.
  • TDSR caps total monthly debt at 55% of gross income; MSR caps HDB/EC housing repayment at 30%. Both limit your maximum loan.
  • Bank rates move with SORA after any fixed period — model a higher rate to stress-test affordability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is a home loan instalment calculated in Singapore?

Using the reducing-balance formula P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1). Bank loans are usually fixed for 1–3 years, then float with SORA; HDB loans use a fixed 2.6% concessionary rate.

What is the current home loan rate in Singapore?

HDB concessionary loans are fixed at 2.6%. Bank loans are roughly 3–3.5% in 2026, pegged to SORA after any fixed period. MAS manages policy via the exchange rate rather than a set interest rate.

What is TDSR and how does it limit my loan?

The Total Debt Servicing Ratio caps all your monthly debt repayments at 55% of gross income. For HDB flats and ECs, the Mortgage Servicing Ratio also caps housing repayment at 30% of income.

Should I take an HDB loan or a bank loan?

HDB's 2.6% is stable and allows higher LTV for eligible flats. Bank loans can start lower but float with SORA and have stricter LTV. Many buyers refinance from HDB to a bank loan later if rates fall.

Can I prepay or refinance my home loan?

Yes. Banks allow prepayment (watch for lock-in penalties during the fixed period) and many owners refinance every few years to chase lower SORA-linked rates.
