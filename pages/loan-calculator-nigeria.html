Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current loan rate in Nigeria?

Roughly 28%–32% p.a. for consumer loans (CBN MPR 27.5%); NMRC mortgages ≈22–24%.

How is repayment calculated?

Reducing-balance formula. Beware flat-rate quotes — they cost far more than they appear.

Why are rates so high?

The CBN keeps the MPR high to control inflation and support the naira, so bank lending is expensive.

Loan or save and buy?

At ~29%, a loan roughly doubles cost over a few years. Saving and buying outright is usually far cheaper.
