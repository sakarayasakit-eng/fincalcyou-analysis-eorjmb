Frequently Asked Questions

How does reksa dana investing work in Indonesia?

You invest a fixed amount each month into a mutual fund (reksa dana). The fund buys a diversified portfolio, and your returns compound over time. Monthly investing smooths out market ups and downs.

What return can I expect?

The IHSG (Jakarta index) has historically returned about 9–11% per year over the long term. Mixed funds are steadier at 7–9%, money-market funds around 4–6%.

What is the minimum investment?

Apps like Bibit, Bareksa and Ajaib let you start from roughly Rp 10,000–100,000 per month, so you can begin small and increase later.

Are Shariah-compliant funds available?

Yes. Saham syariah and sharia money-market reksa dana are widely offered and screened to avoid non-compliant sectors and interest-based holdings.

Is reksa dana safe?

Equity funds carry market risk and can fall in any given year, but are diversified and regulated by OJK. Money-market reksa dana are much steadier. Match the fund type to your time horizon.
