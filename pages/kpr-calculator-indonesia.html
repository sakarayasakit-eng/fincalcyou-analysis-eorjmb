KPR Subsidi (FLPP) vs commercial KPR

  • KPR Subsidi / FLPP — government-subsidized fixed rate (around 5%) for eligible first-home, lower-income buyers. Much cheaper but income and price caps apply.
  • Commercial KPR — bank SBDK rate (8–12.5%), usually fixed for an intro period then floating. Use the rates above.
  • LTV up to 100% for 2026 under Bank Indonesia macroprudential easing — but a larger down payment lowers your cicilan and total interest.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is KPR cicilan calculated in Indonesia?

Banks use the reducing-balance (anuitas) formula: cicilan = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ ((1+r)ⁿ − 1), where P is the loan, r the monthly rate and n the months. Many KPR packages use a fixed rate for the first few years, then switch to a floating SBDK rate.

What is the current KPR rate in Indonesia?

As of the latest SBDK data (Oct 2025), KPR rates range from about 8.04% (BTN) to 12.5% (Mandiri). The BI-Rate is 4.75%. Floating KPR rates move with the bank's SBDK.

What is KPR Subsidi / FLPP?

A government program offering a fixed subsidized rate (around 5%) to eligible first-time, lower-income buyers, with income and property-price caps. It is far cheaper than commercial KPR if you qualify.

How much down payment (uang muka) do I need?

Bank Indonesia allows up to 100% LTV in 2026, so a 0% down payment is possible in theory, but most buyers still pay 10–20% to reduce the cicilan and total interest.

Can I pay off my KPR early?

Yes, though some banks charge an early-settlement penalty during the fixed-rate period. Even partial prepayments during the floating phase cut years of interest — check your contract's penalty clause first.
