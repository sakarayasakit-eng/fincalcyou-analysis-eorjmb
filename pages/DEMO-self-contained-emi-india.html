How the EMI is calculated

Every Indian bank uses the same reducing-balance formula:

EMI = P × r × (1+r)n ÷ ((1+r)n − 1)

P = loan amount, r = monthly rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = number of months (years × 12).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current home loan rate in India?

Roughly 7.75%–9% p.a. as of April 2026, floating with the RBI repo (5.25%). Your CIBIL score and profile decide the exact rate.

What is the maximum tenure?

Up to 30 years at most banks, subject to your age at maturity. Longer tenure lowers the EMI but raises total interest.

Should I prepay or invest?

Prepaying saves a guaranteed ~8.5%. Investing may earn more (Nifty long-term ≈12%) but carries risk. Compare both with your numbers.
